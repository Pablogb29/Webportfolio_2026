# Vision

## Mission

Create a portfolio that positions Pablo as a credible cybersecurity engineer moving into penetration testing with real evidence, strong judgment, and disciplined execution.

## What Success Looks Like

- A recruiter understands the profile within 10 seconds.
- A technical reviewer sees proof of depth, not generic security buzzwords.
- A hiring manager can map the portfolio to pentesting, security engineering, IAM, and offensive-security-adjacent roles.
- The visual system feels premium, minimal, and operator-grade.
- The site remains easy to maintain as new writeups, case studies, certifications, and projects are added.

## Non-Negotiables

- No fake claims, inflated seniority, or fabricated metrics.
- No substance drift from the current portfolio or resume.
- No childish hacker aesthetics or neon-terminal parody.
- No animation that harms clarity, accessibility, or performance.
- No design decisions without rationale.

## Strategic Outcome

The site should feel like a high-trust artifact from someone who can investigate, document, automate, and communicate security work clearly.

## Primary Audiences

- Recruiters hiring for cybersecurity, pentesting, security engineering, or IAM-adjacent roles.
- Technical interviewers validating practical depth.
- Security managers looking for maturity, trajectory, and signal density.
- Peers reviewing writeups, labs, and projects for credibility.

## Product Thesis

The portfolio should not compete by being louder than other portfolios. It should compete by being clearer, calmer, more evidence-driven, and more professional.
