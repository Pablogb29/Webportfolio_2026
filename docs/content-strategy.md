# Content Strategy

## Source Of Truth Rule

Use the current portfolio, CV, and linked public work as the factual baseline. Editing may improve wording, structure, hierarchy, and emphasis, but must not invent achievements or materially alter the profile.

## Content Goals

- Explain the professional profile quickly.
- Make the Blue Team to Red Team transition believable.
- Turn dense experience into readable evidence.
- Elevate projects from blurbs into proof-rich case studies.
- Make labs and certifications reinforce the story instead of feeling separate.

## Homepage Content Hierarchy

1. Identity and positioning
2. Immediate proof and trust signals
3. Transition narrative and specialization
4. Experience and capability map
5. Featured projects and case studies
6. Labs, writeups, and certification path
7. Contact and opportunity fit

## Copy Rules

- Prefer specifics over adjectives.
- Keep sentences short unless detail adds signal.
- Name tools only when they support a point.
- Distinguish current responsibilities, ongoing preparation, and future goals.
- Convert long paragraphs into scannable structures when possible.

## Proof Ladder

Strongest to weakest:
1. Real work done in a professional setting
2. Public case studies or writeups
3. Thesis or formal education work
4. Certifications completed or actively in progress
5. Aspirational interests

## Section Expectations

- Hero: identity, direction, and CTA clarity.
- About: narrative and differentiators.
- Experience: responsibilities translated into recruiter-readable outcomes.
- Skills: normalized taxonomy rather than a keyword dump.
- Projects: challenge, approach, result, relevance.
- Labs/writeups: learning velocity and methodology.
- Contact: role fit and response confidence.
