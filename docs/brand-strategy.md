# Brand Strategy

## Positioning Statement

Pablo is a cybersecurity engineer with practical IAM and automation experience who is deliberately transitioning toward penetration testing, bringing a defensive systems perspective that strengthens offensive work.

## Core Brand Pillars

- Technical credibility: show real tools, workflows, environments, and outputs.
- Operational maturity: communicate with restraint, precision, and structure.
- Dual perspective: show why Blue Team experience improves Red Team judgment.
- Growth trajectory: present current depth and future direction without overselling.

## Differentiators To Emphasize

- IAM and access-governance experience as a practical security foundation.
- Active Directory focus tied to real attack paths and lab work.
- Security automation work that demonstrates engineering discipline, not only tooling familiarity.
- Case-study and writeup mindset that proves documentation and investigative ability.

## Tone Of Voice

- Concise
- Technical
- Calm
- Specific
- Evidence-led
- Professional, never theatrical

## Messaging Priorities

1. Clarify current role and target direction.
2. Show why the transition to pentesting is credible.
3. Prove depth through projects, labs, case studies, and certifications.
4. Make it easy to contact Pablo for the right opportunities.

## Phrases To Favor

- "Cybersecurity engineer"
- "Transitioning into penetration testing"
- "Active Directory security"
- "Security automation"
- "IAM and access governance"
- "OSCP-oriented practice"
- "Documented, reproducible work"

## Phrases To Avoid

- "Cyber ninja", "ethical hacker expert", or other inflated labels.
- Empty buzzword piles without evidence.
- Overly dramatic threat language.
- Claims of impact or scale that are not documented.

## Trust Signals To Surface

- Named tools and environments when factual.
- Public repositories and writeups.
- Concrete professional responsibilities.
- Thesis work and certification path.
- Clear contact pathways and role fit.
