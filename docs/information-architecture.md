# Information Architecture

## Recommended Sitemap

- / Home
- /projects Project archive
- /case-studies Case study index
- /case-studies/[slug] Detailed security or engineering case studies
- /labs HTB and practical lab work
- /writeups Future writeup index
- /contact Contact and opportunity fit

## Homepage Section Order

1. Hero
2. Trust band
3. About / positioning
4. Experience
5. Skills matrix
6. Featured projects
7. Case studies or writeups preview
8. Education and certifications
9. Contact CTA

## Why This Order

- Hero and trust band reduce ambiguity fast.
- Experience before skills keeps the site evidence-led.
- Projects come before education because proof of practice should outrank credentials.
- Education and certifications should validate trajectory, not carry the site.

## Future Content Extensions

- Add a dedicated blog only when there is enough writing to avoid an empty section.
- Keep writeups and case studies distinct: writeups show hands-on practice, case studies show deeper narrative and judgment.
- Add speaking, services, or research only when real evidence exists.

## Navigation Rules

- Keep top-level nav short and high-value.
- Avoid duplicate routes that fragment authority.
- Ensure every route has a clear purpose and a visible next action.
